# Support DPS

Support DPS creates the kill window.

Damage without pierce, heal cut, armor reduction, or utility often becomes fake pressure.

## Purpose

Support DPS makes allied damage matter.

Possible contributions include:

- pierce
- heal cut / anti-heal
- armor reduction
- slows or holds
- purge
- pressure that keeps targets inside damage
- utility that makes enemy healing/defense weaker or late

## Timing principle

Utility late is often worthless.

If main damage lands before pierce or heal cut, the enemy may survive with defensives, Locus, Guard, Demon, Rootbound, or healing already active.

Sometimes a smaller on-time button is better than a big late E.

## What you watch

- actual damage target
- enemy defensive stack
- whether the clump is real
- whether your E will be late
- whether W/smaller utility can still land on time
- whether your utility is enabling kills or chasing meters

## Common failures

- chasing damage numbers
- casting utility into the wrong clump
- landing pierce after damage
- using E randomly before the engage
- ignoring heal-cut requirements
- hitting isolated protected tanks instead of the kill window

## Practice drill

For every engage, answer:

1. Did support utility land before or with damage?
2. Did it hit the actual target?
3. Did it create a kill window?
4. If E was late, was there a better on-time button?

## Minimum value standard

Support DPS is neither normal DPS nor pure support. It must bring utility that helps the zerg convert kills while still adding enough damage, pressure, or disruption to justify a DPS slot.
