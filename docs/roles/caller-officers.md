# Caller / Officers


Callers and officers turn information into risk-managed decisions. The caller chooses the plan; the zerg and officers make the plan survivable.

## Purpose

The caller is not there to micromanage every step. The caller chooses direction, tempo, target logic, and risk. Officers and role leads help players understand how to execute those decisions.

## What good calling uses

- real enemy position, not emotional reports
- kill feed and actual deaths
- cooldown state
- map and terrain
- enemy style and movement
- whether the zerg is healthy and close enough to act

## Officer responsibility

Officers should convert doctrine into simple operational checks:

- Are healers present and mixed correctly?
- Are critical supports covered?
- Are tanks front enough to protect the zerg?
- Are DPS close enough to hit the next turn?
- Is the review focused on one or two habits instead of every mistake?

## Common failures

- calling based on vague reports like "we killed a lot"
- overcorrecting players in the middle of a fight
- ignoring map geometry
- reviewing outcome instead of process
- forcing too much role flex before players have stable reps

## Practice drill

After a fight, pick one call that worked and one call that failed. For each, identify the risk the caller accepted and what the zerg needed to do to mitigate that risk.
