# Engage / Clump Tanks

Engage tanks create or reinforce the moment where the zerg's damage can kill.

## Purpose

Your job is not to chase random kills. Your job is to create a visible, timed, survivable clump/lock so allied DPS can hit together.

A good engage tank:

- creates or reinforces a clump before damage lands
- survives the engage path
- understands when to hold enemies in damage
- does not default into DTank play
- exits or transitions after the engage instead of feeding

## Where you stand

You usually play near the front or on a controlled angle. You are not always directly in the enemy. Your position should let you threaten enemy movement while still receiving support.

## Timing

If the clump appears after DPS needed to aim, it is late.

Correct sequence:

1. caller identifies the window
2. tank is already moving into position
3. clump/hold exists before or at zero
4. pierce/heal cut/support utility aligns
5. damage lands while enemies are controlled
6. tank exits or transitions to stopper/control work

## Hold vs pull

Not every engage should drag enemies. Sometimes holding enemies in place is better because allied AoE, ranged pressure, or follow-up needs targets to remain inside the damage.

## Common failures

- late pull
- clumping after DPS already cast
- dragging enemies out of allied AoE
- diving too deep without an exit
- using greed tools that remove tank survivability
- leaving before the zerg is safe

## Practice drill

Pause two seconds before every engage and ask:

> Is the engage tank already close enough to create the clump?

If the tank only starts moving at the countdown, the engage is probably late.

## Defensive flexibility after the engage

An engage tank should not **default into DTank play** or abandon engage timing early. Their main job is to create, hold, or enable the offensive window.

That does not mean engage tanks can never make defensive plays. After their engage cooldown is spent, or in emergency fight states, an engage tank may switch into stopping, disrupting, peeling, or controlling enemy pressure if doing so protects the zerg and does not ruin the next engage cycle.

Good rule:

> Engage first when your engage is the win condition. Switch to defensive disruption only when cooldown state, fight state, or emergency danger makes it correct.

This is why experienced players may use engage-control tools defensively in extreme situations, while newer engage tanks should avoid turning every fight into improvised DTank play.
