## Purpose

Apply front-to-back pressure, punish low targets, and avoid blind deep first-engage dives.

## Positioning

Positioning is role-specific, but the universal test is: **can you affect the fight when your role is needed?** If not, you are too far, too safe, too late, or on the wrong angle.

## Before the engage

- Watch enemy name tags and movement, not only the caller icon.
- Prepare the ability or defensive that creates your role's value.
- Start moving before the countdown if you need time to arrive.

## During engage / enemy engage

- Execute the role job first; do not chase unrelated value.
- If enemy damage lands elsewhere and you are safe, prepare to counter or pressure.
- If you are caught and fragile, assume the engage is real and use defensive tools.

## Between engages

- Stay in formation.
- Use Q/W pressure, heals, scouting, or support value where appropriate.
- Do not drift into a pile or one screen away.

## Common failures

- Being late because you waited for a perfect call or perfect clump.
- Playing safe in a way that removes your role from the fight.
- Using your strongest cooldown randomly, then missing the real window.
- Treating scoreboard numbers as more important than role value.

## Practice habit

Review one fight and mark every moment where this role was either **in position**, **late**, **too safe**, or **creating value**.

## Batch 4 melee-burst reinforcement

AOU Ep. 17 reinforces that melee burst must be punctual. If a melee burst player is two or three seconds late to the main clump, they may need to hit a safer side clump or alternate real target instead of diving into the enemy counter-engage point.
