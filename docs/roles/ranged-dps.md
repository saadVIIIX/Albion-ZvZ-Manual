# Ranged DPS

Ranged DPS is not just pressing E on a countdown.

Good ranged DPS controls space, pressures between engages, and finds value when no perfect clump is handed to them.

## Purpose

Ranged DPS creates pressure at distance and converts clumps, low targets, and enemy movement mistakes into kills.

## Where you stand

You should be close enough to hit the next window but not stacked inside friendly players.

Good ranged positioning:

- avoids walls/corners that create friendly clumps
- maintains effective range
- keeps an escape direction
- stays close enough to turn after enemy cooldowns
- reads where enemies will move, not only where they are now

## Timing

Damage must land on the window.

If your spell has cast time or travel time, starting at zero is often late. The correct cast starts before the damage should land.

## Between engages

Ranged DPS should not disappear between major E cooldowns. A useful ranged DPS player keeps creating value through:

- Q/W pressure
- small-clump hits
- low-target conversion
- safe auto attacks when they do not trap you
- defensive area denial or path control when your kit allows it
- pressure on enemies entering friendly backline space

Many fights are won by pressure between major cooldowns.

## Common failures

- standing too far back to hit the counter
- waiting forever for a perfect clump
- panic-walking with full HP after enemy cooldowns
- casting after support utility has expired
- walking into the target before casting
- throwing damage in the called direction instead of the real clump

## Practice drill

For every engage, mark:

1. clump/lock appears
2. support utility lands
3. main damage lands

If these happen in the wrong order, the engage becomes weak even if the build is good.
