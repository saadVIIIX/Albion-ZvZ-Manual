# Positional Pressure Without an Engage

## Situation

The enemy wants to walk out, rotate, take a wall, leave an objective, or reset. Your zerg does not have a perfect engage.

## Correct response

Use position, Q/W pressure, tank presence, slows, firewalls, and support pressure to make enemy movement uncomfortable.

You do not always need a full bomb to win space.

## What positional pressure can do

- force enemies away from good terrain
- split enemy movement
- make healers work
- bait defensive cooldowns
- create smaller clumps
- trap enemies against walls or chokes
- prevent free resets

## Common failure

Players wait for a perfect engage while the enemy walks out for free. No one pressures, no one occupies the route, and no one turns small opportunities into value.

## Review questions

1. What route did enemy want?
2. Who could pressure that route safely?
3. Did tanks/supports occupy useful space?
4. Did DPS Q/W pressure happen?
5. Did the zerg wait for perfection and lose position?

## Drill

Pick a 10-second period where no major engage happens. Ask who created space or pressure without E.
