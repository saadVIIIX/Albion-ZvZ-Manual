# Healer Deep Dive

Episode 19 focuses on healers.

The central lesson is that healing is not only clump healing. A healer must understand target selection, forward movement, mana, defensive tradeoffs, and the difference between Holy, Nature, and pocket healing jobs.

## Healer families discussed

| Family | Public role |
|---|---|
| Holy burst / direct healing | emergency stabilization and targeted saving |
| Nature / HoT healing | sustained coverage, recovery, flank/route support |
| Pocket healing | keeping a high-value player alive |

## Fallen and Holy logic

Fallen-style healing is valuable because it provides large area recovery, but the episode strongly warns against reducing Holy to only AoE clump healing.

A key recovered point:

> Almost everything in ZvZ healing already heals clumps. What is often missing is reliable single-target saving.

## Single-target Holy Q argument

The episode makes a strong case for single-target Holy Q because:

- clumps already receive many AoE heals
- isolated or low-value-target players may need precise saving
- AoE Q can heal the wrong players due to target distribution
- tanks/frontliners may fall back low while DPS runs forward, and targeted healing can save them

Public caution: exact current Q behavior and values should be verified before stable build claims.

## Nature and sustain logic

Nature healing is not filler.

Nature / HoT / sustain healers provide:

- recovery after first engage
- sustained route support
- flank/skirmish healing
- healing zones that let the zerg remain active
- stabilization when fights do not end in one clump

The public manual should keep reinforcing that a zerg needs both Holy and Nature-style value.

## Heal forward rule

Healers should usually place healing in the direction the zerg is moving or where useful space is being held.

Healing behind the zerg can accidentally teach players to walk backward and surrender space.

Public lesson:

> Heal the route and danger area that lets the next correct movement happen.

## Mana and defensive tradeoffs

Healers need both:

- mana regeneration
- defensive options

Common patterns discussed include mana boots/capes/helmets and defensive helmets/boots. Exact item choices remain patch-sensitive.

## Pocket healing

Pocket healing exists to keep a high-value player alive, such as a caller or critical role.

Public caution:

- do not overexpand pocket healing into general doctrine without enough source material
- keep it as a specialized role family

## Review questions

- Did the healer heal the real danger area?
- Did the healer save low-value-target players precisely when needed?
- Did healing placement encourage forward/useful movement?
- Did Nature/sustain value help recovery and flanks?
- Did the healer have enough mana and defensives to keep functioning?
- Did the healer watch enemy movement, not only party frames?
