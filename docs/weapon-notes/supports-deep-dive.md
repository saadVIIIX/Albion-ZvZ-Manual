# Supports Deep Dive

Episode 20 focuses on supports.

The central lesson is that supports are direct counters to enemy pressure. A support who reacts only after damage lands is usually too late.

## Support families reinforced

| Family | Main job |
|---|---|
| Damage mitigation | reduce or absorb incoming damage |
| Mobility support | keep the zerg moving, rotating, and crossing danger |
| Enemy disruption | stop, purge, interrupt, cleanse, slow, or delay enemy plans |

## Anticipation rule

Supports must anticipate enemy movement.

If the enemy engages and the support only starts reacting after damage lands, tools like Enigmatic, Locus, or Great Arcane may land too late.

Supports should read:

- enemy name tags
- bomb squads on side angles
- tank movement
- DPS stacking
- sudden rotations
- off-cooldown timers
- where the friendly zerg is vulnerable

## Supports are high-value targets

A support caught in the same clump as the zerg may be unable to help the zerg.

Public lesson:

> Supports must position close enough to counter the enemy, but not so carelessly that they become part of the clump they were supposed to save.

## Enigmatic logic

Enigmatic is framed as strong anti-burst support because it repeatedly applies shielding during a danger window.

Common mistakes:

- channeling too late
- shielding safe space
- missing side-angle bomb squads
- acting only on the caller's front rather than reading the whole fight

## Locus logic

Locus is valuable because cleanse/mobility support can both remove pressure and keep the zerg rotating together.

Important public framing:

- cleanse can be defensive
- cleanse can also be mobility support
- exact speed values/current behavior are patch-sensitive
- Locus should answer real danger, not safe space

## Great Arcane / Arcane logic

Great Arcane and Arcane-family tools are direct-counter supports. Their value depends on timing and enemy-read discipline.

They should stop the enemy plan before the enemy has already completed it.

## Occult / mobility support logic

Occult-style mobility support helps rotations, crossings, bomb-squad movement, or zerg speed.

Public caution:

- mobility support is not automatically defensive
- it must support the caller's movement plan and the zerg's fight shape

## Save priority: tanks

When a support must choose where to place defensive value, tanks often deserve priority because if the tank layer dies, the next people exposed are supports and backline roles.

Public lesson:

> Supports in plate often see tank danger earlier than cloth healers. They should help keep the tank layer alive when possible.

## Always do something

Supports should not idle when no engage is happening.

They can:

- scan walls and angles
- find bomb squads
- mark enemy DPS/tanks mentally
- prepare defensive positioning
- track enemy cooldown patterns
- choose where their next cooldown would matter

## Review questions

- Did the support anticipate or react late?
- Did they watch side angles and bomb squads?
- Did their cooldown land on real danger?
- Did they save tanks/frontline when needed?
- Did they stay close enough to help without getting caught?
- Did they prepare while the fight looked quiet?
