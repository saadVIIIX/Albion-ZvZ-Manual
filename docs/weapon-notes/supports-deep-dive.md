# Support Tool Notes

Support tools are direct answers to enemy pressure.

## Support functions

| Function | What it solves |
|---|---|
| Damage mitigation | helps the zerg survive a danger window |
| Mobility support | helps the zerg rotate, cross, or disengage as a unit |
| Enemy disruption | interrupts, purges, slows, or delays enemy plans |
| Tank/front protection | keeps the protective layer alive long enough to matter |

## Anticipation rule

Support tools should be prepared before the enemy damage lands. A perfect defensive placed after the danger has passed is usually too late.

Supports should watch:

- enemy name tags
- bomb squads on side angles
- tank movement
- DPS stacking
- sudden rotations
- off-cooldown patterns
- where the friendly zerg is vulnerable

## Positioning rule

Supports must be close enough to counter enemy pressure, but not so close that they become part of the clump they were supposed to save.

## Save priority

When a support must choose where to place defensive value, the tank/frontline layer often deserves attention. If the players holding danger collapse, healers and DPS become exposed next.

## Review questions

- Did the support anticipate or react late?
- Did they watch side angles and bomb squads?
- Did their cooldown land on real danger?
- Did they help tanks/frontline when needed?
- Did they stay close enough to help without getting caught?
