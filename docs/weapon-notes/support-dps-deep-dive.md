# Support DPS Deep Dive

Episode 18 focuses on support DPS.

The central lesson is that support DPS is not regular DPS and not pure support. It is a damage role that brings utility important enough to justify lower raw damage.

## Definition

Support DPS is:

> a DPS weapon whose main value includes utility that enables the zerg, while still contributing damage.

It should not be judged only by damage meter output.

## Weapon / utility families discussed

| Tool / family | Utility identity |
|---|---|
| Permafrost | stun/root/slow utility plus damage |
| Damnation | large reliable pierce plus curse damage |
| Spirit Hunter | pierce plus damage |
| Carving | pierce utility, more melee/position-dependent |
| Realmbreaker | pierce / pressure / clump-value hybrid depending on setup |
| Bow of Badon | channel interruption / disruption logic |
| Dawnsong | anti-heal plus fire kit value; current status patch-sensitive |
| Daybreaker | purge concept, but weak public viability warning |

## Permafrost rule

Permafrost is a utility DPS weapon.

If a player turns it into a greed-damage weapon by cutting utility/survival tools, they may destroy the reason the weapon was picked.

Public lesson:

> If the weapon is chosen for utility, gear and ability choices should strengthen that utility job.

## Pierce weapons

Damnation, Spirit Hunter, Carving, and similar tools should be judged by whether they make allied damage matter.

A pierce that lands late or on the wrong clump can look active but fail its job.

Important timing rule:

```text
clump / lock → support DPS utility → main damage → enemy recovery
```

## Damnation notes

Damnation has large area reliability. Its E alone may not be the largest pierce, but combining E and W can create very high pierce value when timed correctly.

Public caution:

- exact pierce values are patch-sensitive
- E/W sequence details should be verified before stable claims
- the role lesson is timing and alignment, not permanent numbers

## Support DPS gear rule

The gear should help the utility role.

Do not blindly add damage modifiers if they provide less value than survivability, cast reliability, positioning, or utility completion.

Example public lesson:

> A support DPS that dies before utility lands is not doing support and not doing DPS.

## Timing and exit rule

Support DPS should often prepare early, hit on the window, and leave before enemy counter-engage.

Do your job and get out.

Waiting to watch the result is a VOD-review habit, not an in-fight habit.

## Review questions

- Did the support DPS utility land before or with allied damage?
- Did it hit the same clump allied damage hit?
- Did the player gear for the utility job?
- Did the player leave after completing the job?
- Did they add enough damage/pressure to justify DPS classification?
- Did they avoid playing like a regular greed DPS?
