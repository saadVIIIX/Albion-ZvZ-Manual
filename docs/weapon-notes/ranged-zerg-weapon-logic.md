# Ranged Zerg Weapon Logic

This page extracts the broad ranged-zerg weapon logic from AO University Ep. 7.

It is not a complete comp sheet. It explains why different ranged tools contribute different kinds of value.

## Ranged zerg identity

Ranged zergs generally value:

- large AoE damage,
- standoff distance,
- area denial,
- Q/W pressure that cleans the backline,
- keeping enemy melee/frontline out of friendly ranged DPS,
- fighting around chokes, rivers, terrain, and controlled spacing.

## Ranged vs melee damage distinction

| Factor | Ranged tendency | Melee tendency |
|---|---|---|
| AoE size | often wider/larger | often smaller or directional |
| Safety | can hit from distance | must expose itself closer |
| Escalation | benefits from large AoE clumps | benefits more from resilience penetration and commit timing |
| Pressure | can use Q/W to clean backline | often needs commit windows or brawl uptime |

## Main ranged damage tools from the episode

| Tool | Teaching value | Caution |
|---|---|---|
| Brimstone | backbone-style burst, large AoE, non-reflectable hit, useful Q/W package | exact build/ability values are patch-sensitive |
| Siege Bow | huge area denial, repeated ticks, strong Q pressure, delays enemy counter | channel/standing time creates risk; reflect/meta status patch-sensitive |
| Energy Shaper | long-line area denial and standoff pressure | requires strong targeting; limited stacking value |
| Longbow | lower cooldown pressure/conversion weapon | W/E dependency and Q/W limitations must be understood |
| Mistpiercer | exciting line-damage concept | episode frames it as heavily limited compared with stronger ranged options |
| Permafrost | support damage plus clump control | lower raw damage than pure burst but adds utility |
| Dawnsong | support damage plus anti-heal pressure | timing with damage matters more than meter chasing |
| Bow of Badon | silence/interrupt and area denial support damage | requires line of sight and proper clump/control support |

## Ranged healer/support logic

Ranged zergs also need ranged-compatible healers and supports.

Recovered examples:

- Fallen for burst healing and cleanse.
- Wild/Rampant-style Nature for ranged or path-based healing zones.
- Occult for mobility support.
- Great Arcane-style tools for choke/flood denial.
- Icicle for kite/control.
- Locus and Enigmatic as important defensive layers.
- Damnation / ranged pierce as more compatible with ranged zergs than melee-only pierce tools.

## Ranged playstyle

Common ranged-zerg behaviors:

- hold chokes,
- kite from choke to choke,
- use crescent formations,
- keep standoff distance,
- punish enemies who try to flood into friendly backline.

## Weaknesses

Ranged zergs can be vulnerable because:

- many ranged DPS are cloth-heavy,
- many casts/channels can be interrupted,
- positioning and clump-finding require high discipline,
- if enemy melee gets inside, ranged DPS can collapse quickly.
