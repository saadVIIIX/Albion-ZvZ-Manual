# Melee DoT / Pressure DPS Deep Dive

Episode 16 focuses on melee damage-over-time DPS.

The central lesson is that damage over time is not glorious, but it can be strategically decisive because it strains enemy healers, splits attention, burns mana, and shapes enemy movement.

## Weapons and concepts discussed

| Weapon / concept | Main public lesson |
|---|---|
| Halberd | classic melee DoT pressure; spreads bleeds and healing reduction |
| Infernal Scythe | hybrid pressure/execute; more burst than pure Halberd |
| Great Axe | historical DoT/pressure example, current use patch-sensitive |
| Bear Paws | bleed/burst hybrid |
| Clarent / Kingmaker-style swords | low-cooldown repeated pressure can act like DoT if played that way |
| Stalker Jacket comparison | shows why damage-over-time pressure matters, but sacrifices survivability |

## Why DoT matters

DoT/pressure damage usually does not create clean highlights.

Its value is in accumulated stress:

- forces healers to keep looking away from the main bomb
- drains enemy healer mana over time
- makes healers reposition more often
- punishes clumped melee meta behavior
- creates psychological pressure
- makes enemy players back up from useful angles
- can drive enemies toward bad terrain or chokes

## Healer-pressure theory

A pressure player can make enemy healers constantly choose:

- heal the pressure target
- save cooldowns for the main engage
- step forward into danger
- step back and abandon the front
- spend mana continuously
- lose tempo and awareness

This is why pressure can matter even when it does not directly secure the kill.

## Halberd logic

Halberd is framed as the classic melee DoT weapon.

Public lessons:

- AoE Q is favored for spreading bleeds broadly
- Internal Bleeding-style W adds another pressure layer
- E spreads bleeds across nearby players
- fully stacked bleeds add healing-pressure value
- the player should focus on uptime and spread, not chasing one low target

Patch caution: exact healing-reduction values and current ability behavior should be verified.

## Infernal Scythe logic

Infernal Scythe is a hybrid.

It can contribute pressure through axe Q/W logic while also providing execute or burst-like value.

Public framing:

> Infernal Scythe sits between pressure and finisher. It should not be judged only as a pure bloodletter-style execute.

## Sword pressure logic

Clarent / Kingmaker-style examples are discussed as possible pressure hybrids when low-cooldown E and Blade Cyclone-style W are used aggressively.

Public framing:

> A weapon can become pressure-oriented by how the whole kit is played, not only by the weapon name.

Patch caution: sword W/E details are patch-sensitive.

## Playstyle rules

### Trade your mana for enemy healer mana

If you have mana, use your Q/W/E pressure. Your goal is to make enemy healers spend more attention and mana than they want.

### Stay active inside enemy space

The longer you survive while applying pressure, the more value you create.

### Do not chase one kill

If one low player runs away, do not abandon pressure on the whole enemy body unless the kill is guaranteed and safe.

### Be annoying enough to split attention

If enemy healers, supports, or DPS are focusing on you, they are not focusing fully on the main fight.

### Drive the bus

Pressure from the side can make enemies back away from one angle and drift toward a worse one. This can push them toward chokes, walls, or bad terrain.

## Review questions

- Did the melee DoT player keep pressure uptime?
- Did they spread pressure instead of chasing one target?
- Did they stay alive long enough for DoT to matter?
- Did they pressure soft targets when available?
- Did they help shape enemy movement?
