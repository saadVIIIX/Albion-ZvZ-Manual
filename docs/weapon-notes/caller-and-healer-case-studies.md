# Caller and Healer Case Studies

This page captures the build-synergy case-study logic from AO University Ep. 5.

## Soul Scythe caller logic

The Soul Scythe caller case study is not just about one old weapon choice. It teaches caller build priorities.

### Caller requirements

A caller needs:

- mobility
- vision
- standoff distance
- survival
- cooldown timing that lets the zerg read the engage
- enough safety to keep calling after the enemy counter-engages

### Why mobility matters

Caller mobility is not personal greed. It lets the caller:

- see multiple fronts,
- move between angles,
- avoid becoming the enemy's free counter target,
- keep enough distance to read the fight,
- survive long enough to make the next decision.

### Soul Scythe teaching value

Soul Scythe-style caller logic emphasizes:

- Q/W mobility,
- ranged visual engage marker,
- standoff engage identity,
- Judicator Armor synergy with pocket healing,
- cooldown advantage from Refreshing Sprint-like boot logic.

### Public-manual caution

This is a caller-build case study, not a universal command that every caller must play Soul Scythe forever. The transferable lesson is:

> A caller build must support the caller's information, movement, survival, and communication job.

## Fallen healer case study

Fallen is used in the episode as the healer case study because it clearly shows role requirement, synergy, and defensives.

### Healer requirements

A healer needs:

- enough healing output,
- enough survivability,
- mobility to reach the danger area,
- W/armor choices that support the actual healer job,
- positioning that lets the healer save the fight, not only themselves.

### Holy Q/W logic

The episode distinguishes between different Holy tools depending on whether the healer is acting as a pocket healer or a general zerg healer.

Public wording should avoid pretending one ability is always correct. The better rule is:

> The ability should match the healer's actual job in the fight.

### Cloth vs heavier defensive choices

Cloth increases healing potential. Heavier armor can increase survival or pocket-healer durability. The trade-off should be intentional.
