# Zerg Heart and Shield: Healer and Support Families

Episode 11 frames healers as the zerg's heart and supports as the zerg's shield.

The key correction is that healers and supports should be split by strategic job, not only by weapon.

## Healer families

| Healer family | Main job | Example weapons / tools |
|---|---|---|
| Burst healer | emergency healing on clumps and danger areas | Fallen, Hallowfall, Rampant, 1h Nature |
| HoT / sustain healer | flank sustain, recovery, long-fight stabilization | Wild, Blight, Rampant, Great Holy as sustain concept, Redemption |
| Pocket healer | keeping a specific high-value player alive | Divine-style shielding, Great Nature, Lifetouch, Ironroot, 1h Holy |

## Burst healer doctrine

Burst healers must understand where enemy damage will land. They cannot simply stand far back and react after the zerg is already dying.

Key points:

- position close enough to reach likely clumps
- watch the enemy, not only party frames
- use tools like Knight Helmet or cleanse hoods to keep the heal available
- cleric robe is emphasized because the healer's job is to get the heal off safely

## HoT / sustain healer doctrine

HoT healers are not failed burst healers. Their job is sustained recovery, flank support, and stabilization after pressure.

Key points:

- flanks take skirmish damage and need sustain
- sustain healers should stay mobile
- do not run into the center trying to burst-heal clumps if their kit is not suited for that moment
- stabilizing after enemy damage often means giving HoT/sustain healers time to top people off

The public lesson is simple: **Nature is not filler**.

## Pocket healer doctrine

Pocket healers are responsible for keeping a high-value player alive. Divine-style shielding is the clean example in the role model, but other targeted or protective healing tools can function as pocket-healing concepts depending on the comp.

This role should not be overexpanded in the public manual until more role material is processed.

## Support families

| Support family | Main job | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Damage mitigation | help the zerg absorb incoming damage | Enigmatic, older Great Arcane framing, Black Monk Staff concept, Oathkeepers |
| Mobility support | let the zerg move, rotate, cross chokes, or speed up | Locus, Occult, cleanse-speed logic, Oathkeepers as shielding / mobility-support overlap |
| Enemy disruptor | slow, stop, purge, interrupt, or delay enemy plans | Icicle, 1h Arcane, Lifecurse, Great Arcane after rework |

## Damage mitigation supports

Damage mitigation helps the zerg absorb damage rather than stopping every enemy cast.

Examples include:

- Enigmatic-style shielding
- older Great Arcane mitigation framing
- Black Monk Staff as a conceptual damage-reduction example, with low-use / patch-sensitive caution
- Oathkeepers-style shielding and resistance support

## Mobility supports

Mobility supports are not just "defensives." They allow the zerg to move through danger and win rotations.

Examples include:

- Locus for cleansing movement, especially through chokes
- Occult for speeding rotations and bomb-squad movement
- Arcane cleanse-speed use on the backline during rotations
- Oathkeepers as an overlap tool when shielding and movement support both matter

## Enemy disruptors

Enemy disruptors prevent or weaken enemy engages.

Examples include:

- Icicle
- 1h Arcane
- Lifecurse
- Great Arcane after its rework, with patch-sensitive caution
